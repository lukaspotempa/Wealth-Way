# Title: Investment Game
# Gameflow 
- a turnbased game, where every player can roll a dice each turn and their character moves to the respectable tile
- At the start of the game, each player has $1000 which he can spend in the investment market.
Each player can decide on what he
- fields are divided in 4 categories:
-- investment action; market event; income; negative events (short time spendings, where cash is needed); shop event
- each time a players turn begins he will be granted %2 of his current money on top (in german its called tagesgeldzins)
# Event description:
- investment action:
Every player (not the dicer but every player) get's the chance to invest their money in the stock market. An UI component will open where each player is displayed with (the same) options to invest into ETFs, stocks and bonds. Create categorie buttons for each and if a category is clicked, all investment options (for e.g. all stocks) will display. The player sees the trend data for each investment option (for e.g. red arrow down -0.14% for a stock means last trend was negative). If an investment option is clicked, the details for this with past data will be shown (as normal for stocks etc) with a graph and timeline
based on the round count, more and more categories and investment options will be added.
each game starts with the ETF as the first unlocked investment option and after a certain amount of rounds (not turns but whole rounds. If every player reaches the "starting point" the number is incremented) more and more is unlocked.
- market event:
a random market event will occur. This event is directly connected to the available investment options. if the players cannot buy single stocks (only etfs for e.g.) then the event will not be focused on a single company (for e.g.: apple moves chip production to the us entirely. This would be an example for an event influencing a single stock). Rather we opt for random events in the unlocked categories. For e.g: round 1 an event is triggered: only etfs are unlocked so we trigger an event which would directly impact the ETFs (negatively but maybe also positively)
- income: A simple event which gives the player who lands on the field a flat amount of money based on a random event. For e.g. grandma gave the player some money. The player "ages" according to the rounds he played (not turns). So for e.g. a player in round 1 will be young. So an event where his mum will gift him $50 is likely. However later on, round 5/10 the player will get a different event, for e.g. he got a bonus at work for doing good work. The flat amount may differ on round progress.
- negative events: events which negatively impact the player who landed on the field. For e.g. he had a pipe burst and has to pay $2000 if the player does not hold this amount of money the game is presumably lost. The last resort is to sell his investments (a gui would appear where he is prompted to either sell or give up).
- shop events: Since the player holds flat money, the player can invest into short term gains. These gains may be some which impact the turn directly. For this I want you to invent some idea with a short term gain. It's important that the player will not influence other players negatively (for e.g. block investment opportunity) but also simply giving the player the ability to move further (double dice) wouldn't be sufficient as this wouldn't give any gain to the player
